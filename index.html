About Recycling process


In India, municipal corporations play a pivotal role in the recycling process of waste by implementing waste segregation, collection, and treatment initiatives. The process begins with door-to-door collection of waste, which is then segregated into biodegradable, recyclable, and non-recyclable categories at the source or designated facilities. Organic waste is often processed into compost or biogas, while recyclable materials like paper, plastic, glass, and metal are sent to authorized recycling units. Many municipalities also collaborate with informal waste pickers and recycling companies to enhance efficiency and recycling rates. Efforts are being made to promote awareness among citizens about the importance of waste segregation and recycling to reduce landfill dependency and encourage sustainable waste management practices.
